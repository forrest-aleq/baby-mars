# Story 22: StorageCorner Real Estate Investment - Expense Reporting & Clarifications
## **Difficulty Level: 1 (Medium)**

### Sarah's Monthly Expense Clarification Marathon

Sarah Chen settles into her workstation at 7:30 AM on the first Tuesday of December, knowing that the next six hours will be consumed by one of the most tedious yet critical processes in StorageCorner's monthly financial cycle: chasing down expense clarifications from property managers across 47 properties who have submitted receipts, invoices, and expense reports with varying degrees of completeness and accuracy. As Expense Coordinator, Sarah serves as the quality control gateway between property-level spending and corporate financial reporting - ensuring every dollar is properly categorized, justified, and compliant before flowing into the company's books.

Her desk setup reflects the multi-channel nature of expense coordination: laptop displaying email queues, printed expense reports with highlighted discrepancies, smartphone for direct manager calls, and a physical filing system for documentation that requires wet signatures. Sarah knows that successful expense coordination requires equal parts detective work, diplomacy, and persistence - transforming incomplete expense submissions into audit-ready financial records that satisfy both accounting standards and investor reporting requirements.
