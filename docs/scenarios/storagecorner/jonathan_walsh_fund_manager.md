# Story 19: StorageCorner Real Estate Investment - Monthly Investor Distributions & Cash Flow Management
## **Difficulty Level: 3 (Very Hard)**

### Jonathan's Investor Relations Balance Act

Jonathan Walsh arrives at StorageCorner's headquarters at 7:30 AM on the last Tuesday of November, carrying the weight of managing one of the most complex and sensitive aspects of real estate fund management: calculating and executing monthly investor distributions while ensuring adequate cash flow for operations, debt service, and capital requirements. As Fund Manager overseeing investor relations for StorageCorner's $89M self-storage portfolio, Jonathan must navigate the delicate balance between investor expectations, operational cash needs, and regulatory compliance across multiple investment structures and investor classes.

His office reflects the complexity of modern real estate fund management: dual monitors displaying cash flow models and investor databases, printed partnership agreements with highlighted distribution waterfall clauses, and detailed Excel models that calculate distributions for 47 different investors across 3 investment structures. Jonathan knows that distribution decisions affect not just investor satisfaction, but the fund's ability to maintain operations, service debt, and pursue growth opportunities.

**Step 2: Preferred Return Calculation**

• **Annual Preferred Return:** 8% = $2,296,000 annually
• **Monthly Preferred Return:** $2,296,000 ÷ 12 = $191,333

**Step 3: Excess Return Analysis**
• **Available Distribution:** $364,600
• **Required Preferred Return:** $191,333
• **Excess Over Preferred:** $364,600 - $191,333 = $173,267

**Step 4: Carried Interest Calculation**
• **GP Carried Interest:** $173,267 × 20% = $34,653
• **Net to Investors:** $364,600 - $34,653 = $329,947
• **Distribution Rate:** $329,947 ÷ $28.7M = 1.15% monthly (13.8% annualized)
